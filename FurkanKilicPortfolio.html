Portfolio

Angry – Monochrome Color Composition

Monochrome Color Study | 2024

Goal: To explore how a single color—in this case, red—can visually express the intense emotion of anger using only tone variations.

Process: The chosen word was Angry. Based on the emotional associations of red—such as aggression, tension, and heat—I developed a composition using only different shades of red. No additional colors were used, in line with the project brief. A mood board guided the emotional tone, and the final composition was built through experimentation with form, rhythm, and contrast to evoke a sense of inner chaos and tension.

Outcome: The resulting design expresses anger through bold shapes, sharp contrasts, and dynamic composition—all in red tones. It demonstrates how a single color, when used thoughtfully, can powerfully convey complex emotional states.

Grocery Gourmet – Greengrocery Website Design

Web Design & Branding | 2024

Goal: To design an interactive, user-friendly, and visually balanced web interface for Grocery Gourmet, a fictional brand I created. The aim of this project was to develop my ability to think in sequences and plan user interaction effectively.

Process: The website consists of five main pages: Home, Products, About Us, Contact, and Online Help. To ensure a clear and intuitive user experience, I started with moodboards, sketches, and wireframes. The design process was guided by key visual principles such as hierarchy, alignment, contrast, and proximity. For the Grocery Gourmet brand, I developed a visual style that conveys freshness and quality, using modern typography, custom illustrations, and a carefully selected color palette to strengthen the brand identity.

Outcome: The final design presents a shopping experience that is both aesthetic and functional, offering a unique and memorable user interface. The entire project was created in Adobe Illustrator at 1920×1080 px resolution, with all text outlined. Grocery Gourmet became a strong example of a contemporary digital greengrocery experience and a highlight in my portfolio.

The Honey-Making Process – Illustrated Infographic

Infographic & Illustration | 2025

Goal: To visually explain the complex and natural process of honey production in a clear, educational, and aesthetically engaging way using original illustrations.

Process: After selecting the honey-making process, I broke it down into seven main steps—from flower pollination to honey storage in the hive. I conducted detailed research to understand each stage clearly. All visual elements were sketched and digitally illustrated by me in Adobe Illustrator—without using any stock images. The visual flow was carefully planned using original icons, a consistent color palette, and a clear hierarchy to ensure both clarity and storytelling.

Outcome: The final infographic, designed in 1080×1920 px vertical format, combines scientific accuracy with creative visual storytelling. By illustrating every element myself, I aimed to make the process feel more authentic, cohesive, and memorable for the viewer. The result is an engaging educational tool suitable for students and general audiences alike.
